
#### What is a basis vector?

There are two basis vectors in any coordinate system: **î**  &  **ĵ**

- **î**  = The unit moving along the X axis.
- **ĵ** = The unit moving along the Y axis.

Vectors should be thought of as Scalars, these stretch and scale to a coordinate in space, and the outcome of this would be the sum of two scaled vectors.

#### What is a linear combination?

Any time two vectors are scaled and added, this is called the linear combination of two vectors. 

![[3b1b - Linear Combinations.png]]
The total number of linear combinations is called the span. If a vector can be removed without affecting the span, this would be called linear dependency. In reverse, this would be called linear independency. This is the same for both two and three dimensional vectors.

### Summary

This lecture covered linear combinations, dependency, and span. 

It is important to understand that vectors can be manipulated, both in two and three dimensions. Their span is the total number of combinations a set of vectors can reach, for example, how many different vectors the set points can reach, specifically the surrounding vectors.
