
A vector is an ordered list of numbers, when looked at from a Computer Science perspective.

However a vector can be anything where vectors can be added or multiplied by a scalar, this typically refers to a point in space or on a coordinate grid.

#### What is the origin?

The place of intersection is the origin, and this is the root of all vectors. It can be seen as coordinates on how to get from origin to a point on the grid.

#### What is Vector multiplication, and what are Scalars?

Vector multiplication refers to extending or shrining the vector, depending on the input. eg: 'V * 1/3' would equal 1/3 the size of the original vector. 

- Scalar: the number eg: (1/3)
- Scaling: the operation.
- Result: a new vector, either longer or shorter. 

Most linear algebra topics revolve around vector addition, and scalar multiplication.



### Summary

Vectors are an ordered list of numbers used to describe space, they can be added and multiplied (scalar multiplication), and are used in Physics, Mathematics, and Computer Science, more commonly in Machine Learning. 

The can be both two and three dimensional and are important factors in gathering a full picture of a space, or data.
